Leadership Development<


Leadership Advisory Board: JMG students had the opportunity to teach a leadership lessson to Elementary Advisory Board members. JMG students taught and modeled GNAP, played a few games to demonstrate the importance of kindness, and held small group discussions with students regarding the topic of kindness.




LEAD Conference: We traveled to Helena, MT to compete among many other schools around the state. We took first place in creating the Logo and our fellow JMG student, James McAlister, earned himself a trip to Washington, D.C. by entering in the Leadership Letter event.

Career Preparation




Career Planning: Took multiple assessments to determine what career fields best suit our interests, skills, and personalities.




Myers Briggs: Completed the Myers Briggs Personality Profile which shows how we view the world and what steps to take when problem solving as well as how one interacts with others.




True Colors: Our initial personality profile where individuals learn how we relate to and interact with other people as well as how this relates to the workplace. The profile reveals how we see ourselves versus how others see us.

Civic Responsibility




Blood Drive: Volunteered for the Red Cross Blood Drive by checking in and provided food and drinks to those who got their blood drawn.





Park Manor: We helped serve food and drinks and bus tables to the residents at Park Manor Assisted Living Home.





Book Nook: Twice a month, JMG members volunteer to read to the students at elementary schools.





Concessions: As a fundraiser and service project, we sold food and drinks to customers at basketball games.



Sletten Cancer Institute: In conjunction with Great Falls High Athletics Department, we made and sold pink headbands in support of cancer. The money raised from headband sales was presented as a $920 check to the Benefis Foundation’s Circle of Hope. While at the facility we toured around the building and learned more about Sletten Cancer Institute.





Radio: Cherry Creek Radio interviewed JMG students about upcoming events and what JMG is about.

Social Awareness



World Homeless Day: To bring awareness to how many homeless students we have in our community, we created a huge world homeless day banner, handed out purple stickers, and encouraged the whole school to wear purple. During an all school assembly, we had guest speakers talk about the importance of World Homeless Day and how we can help those in need.




I and I: I and I stands for Initiation and Installation. During the I & I Ceremony all of the JMG students pitch in to create this wonderful event to celebrate those individuals who have chosen to be in JMG. Students plan and prep the entire event. JMG student speakers share about how they have learned and grown from this program.




Polar Plunge: An organized event to help Special Olympics by raising money to jump into ice cold freezing water. JMG students raised $509 for the worthy cause.




Mardi Gras Party: In celebrating the end of the semester, students and staff gathered to reflect on the highs from the first half of the year. The decor and food was festive and delightful. We also had a mini history lesson Mardi Gras. .




Rainbow Bracelets: We created rainbow bracelets so each member of JMG to come together. We also think that everyone is unique like the colors of a rainbow and together we make something beautiful.
